Highlights

Cooperative Adaptive Cruise Control (CACC)

Cooperative Adaptive Cruise Control (CACC) systems allow connected and automated vehicles to communicate with each other, and coordinate their maneuvers in a form of platoon, where one vehicle follows another with a constant velocity and/or time headway.

  • More

Cooperative Eco-Driving

With advanced Vehicle-to-Infrastructure (V2I) communication technique, "connected eco-driving" provides real-time advices to drivers based on real-time traffic and infrastructure conditions. With connected eco-driving, even greater fuel and emission savings can be achieved without compromising traffic mobility.

  • More

Cooperative Ramp Merging

With advanced Vehicle-to-Vehicle (V2V) and Vehicle-to-Infrastructure (V2I) communication techniques, connected and automated vehicles can schedule their sequence and plan their speed trajectories ahead of time to avoid last-second speed change at the merging point.

  • More

Game Engine Prototyping

Game engine Unity3D is an excellent tool to prototype connected and automated vehicle technology, given its advantages in graphics and visualization, its easiness of integration of driving simulator, and its rich resources on asset store and commUnity3D.

  • More

Human-In-The-Loop Simulation

Driving simulator allows human to drive vehicles modeled in a virtual environment, enabling comparison between proposed connected and automated vehicle technology with human driving behaviors.

  • More

Dyno-In-The-Loop Testing

With Dyno-in-the-loop technology, we are able to conduct test runs in our lab by placing the vehicle on the dynamometer. The driver on the vehicle follows the suggested speed calculated by the microscopic traffic simulator VISSIM, where the real-world traffic is modeled into the network.
