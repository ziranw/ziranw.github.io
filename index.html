I am currently a Principal Researcher at Toyota Motor North America R&D - InfoTech Labs. I received the Ph.D. degree from the University of California, Riverside under the supervision of Dr. Matthew J. Barth. My Ph.D. dissertation is "Developing Agent-Based Distributed Cooperative Vehicle-Infrastructure Systems in the Connected and Automated Vehicle Environment". My research interests include cooperative automated driving, driver behavior modeling with machine learning, and digital twin of intelligent vehicles.

I am an author of 30+ refereed papers and 30+ U.S. patent applications. My research achievements have been demonstrated on Consumer Electronics Show (CES), and acknowledged by National Center for Sustainable Transportation Dissertation Award issued by U.S. Department of Transportation, and by the SAE Vincent Bendix Automotive Electronics Engineering Award (i.e., best paper in 2019) and the SAE Arch T. Colwell Merit Award (i.e., best paper in 2021).

I am serving various roles in multiple academic organizations, including:
-Associate editor of SAE International Journal of Connected and Automated Vehicles, Frontiers in Sustainable Cities-Urban Transportation Systems and Mobility, IEEE International Conference on Intelligent Transportation Systems, and IEEE Intelligent Vehicles Symposium
-Handling editor of SAE Non-Event Technical Papers
-Founding committee chair in the IEEE Intelligent Transportation Systems Society, Technical Committee on Internet of Things in Intelligent Transportation Systems
-Committee member in the IEEE Intelligent Transportation Systems Society, Technical Committee on Cooperative and Connected Vehicles, the IEEE Control Systems Society, Technical Committee on Smart Cities, and the IEEE Industrial Electronics Society, Technical Committee on Industrial Cyber-Physical Systems
-Member in the SAE On Road Automated Driving (ORAD) Simulation Task Force
-Technical program committee member in the 2021 IEEE 94th Vehicular Technology Conference: VTC2021-Fall, and the 2021 IEEE International Conference on Digital Twin and Parallel Intelligence

Highlights

Digital Twin

The Digital Twin is a digital replica of the physical entity in the real world, which leverages big data and cloud computing to benefit the mobility system consisting of human, vehicle, and traffic.

  • More

Cooperative Adaptive Cruise Control (CACC)/Platooning

CACC systems allow connected and automated vehicles to communicate with each other, and coordinate their maneuvers in a form of platoon, where one vehicle follows another with a constant velocity and/or time headway.

  • More

Cooperative Eco-Driving

With advanced Vehicle-to-Infrastructure (V2I) communication technique, "connected eco-driving" provides real-time advices to drivers based on real-time traffic and infrastructure conditions.

  • More

Cooperative Ramp Merging

With advanced Vehicle-to-Everything (V2X) communication techniques, connected and automated vehicles can schedule their sequence and plan their speed trajectories ahead of time to avoid last-second speed change at the merging point.

  • More

Driver Behavior Modeling

Driver behavior (or more generally human factor) plays a crucial role in intelligent vehicles, especially when the driver is operating Advanced Driver Assistance Systems (ADAS).

  • More

Human-In-The-Loop Simulation

Driving simulator allows human to drive vehicles modeled in a virtual environment, enabling comparison between proposed connected and automated vehicle technology with human driving behaviors.
